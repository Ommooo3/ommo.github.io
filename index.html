Who am I?

Hi! I'm Oliver, a 19 year old designer located in the North West of England. I studied graphic design for two years at Blackpool School of Arts, and have done an apprenticeship course with my college for a year. I have a strong passion for content creation, computer science and games.
I like to play Minecraft, Grand Theft Auto V and VALORANT with online friends in my spare time. Following on from my computer science obsessions, I love to mod consoles such as the Nintendo Switch with custom firmware, or tinkering with other devices to put my own unique spin on them and make them feel personalised to my characteristics.


Why graphic design?

I've been interested in graphic design and illustration since the age of 12 following my artistic passion of drawing as a child. I self-taught Photoshop and Premiere Pro to handle basic image manipulation and video edits and continued my interest in graphic design following my Creative iMedia course at high school, where I developed further skills in Photoshop.
My GCSE in Creative iMedia boosted my chances of getting into college to pursue a two year long course in graphic design at Blackpool School of Arts, where I learnt how to use Illustrator, bought an iPad & Apple Pencil combo to begin my journey on Procreate, and spent some sweat and tears to learn InDesign. By the end of the course, I was offered a placement in an apprenticeship which would be supported by my college for an extra year, leading to where I am now.


What apps do I use?

As briefly touched upon in the last paragraph, I am most familiar with the Adobe Suite - more specifically, I am comfortable with: Photoshop, Illustrator, XD, After Effects and Premiere Pro. Thanks to my year-long apprenticeship, I can also efficiently navigate my way through Canva which is a very useful app for creating social media graphics thanks to its intuitive and lightweight UI. In terms of operating systems, I am most comfortable with Windows 10/11. I have a good understanding of MacOS and have dabbled in a little bit of Linux -- specifically Arch Linux.
