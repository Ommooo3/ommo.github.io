Cyberpunk 2077 3D Poster

This project had quite a lengthy design process from start to finish, especially with it being my final college project, but I was fairly proud of the outcome. I illustrated the poster using Procreate, later tracing over the drafted illustration with red and yellow lines in Adobe Illustrator across three different layers. The red lines would indicate where to cut, and the yellow lines would indicate where to engrave the design into the material I am laser cutting onto. This is all thanks to the facilities made available by our college.

We exported the Illustrator file into a specific format and went into CAD software which would recognise our now-exported document full of coloured lines to get the laser cutter ready. Setting out a couple pieces of MDF wood, the laser cutter did its job in roughly an hour, and all the pieces were ready to be glued in. Lastly, we needed to print off the now-finalised illustration in A3, cut out any bleed leftover from the design, and then glue everything together, bringing us to the design we have today.

Personal Branding: Ommo Logos

Listed as one of my more relaxed projects (further emphasised by the fact it was the "calm before the storm" of my upcoming final major college project of that year), these OMMO logos would hypothetically provide me with some identity if I went freelance. Simplistically designed in Illustrator, these colourful logos were fun to design due to their ability to be stacked and act as 'blocks'. Each letter is its own block and can be mixed and matched, though it's very limited due to the simplicity of my online name. I went through lots of different tests to get to the logos I have now, though I cannot find the sheet I drew on with the designs.
